# Customer Journeys: Changi Carbon Calculator

## Overview

Two primary journeys map to the solution's four pillars:

| Persona | Primary Pillars | Key Outcome |
|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Passenger** | Pillar 1 (Calculator) + Pillar 4 (Merchant) | Understands and acts on their complete journey footprint |
| **Sustainability Manager** | Pillar 2 (Stakeholder) + Pillar 3 (Operations) | Accurate data, automated reporting, ACA progression |

# Journey 1: The Passenger

**Persona:** Sarah, 34, Marketing Director based in Singapore. Flies 8-10 times/year for work and leisure. Increasingly climate-conscious but skeptical of greenwashing. Uses Changi App, participates in Changi Rewards.

**Context:** Sarah is flying Singapore â†’ London for a conference. She has a 3-hour buffer at Changi and plans to shop and eat before her flight.

### Current State (Today)

| Touchpoint | Experience | Pain Point |
|------------|------------|------------|
| Books flight | Sees airline's carbon estimate (if any) | Generic number, no context, easy to ignore |
| Changi App check-in | No carbon information | Missed opportunity |

| Touchpoint | Experience | Pillar |

## Key Insights Across Both Journeys

### For Passengers (Sarah)

| Insight | Implication |
|---------|-------------|
| Carbon awareness is episodic, not continuous | Design for moments: check-in, post-purchase, post-departure |
| Guilt without agency leads to disengagement | Always pair information with action options |
| Trust requires transparency | Show methodology, not just numbers |
| Cumulative progress motivates | Build annual tracking, streaks, milestones |

### For Sustainability Managers (David)

| Insight | Implication |
|---------|-------------|
| 80% of time is data wrangling, not analysis | Automate collection and aggregation |
| Stakeholder engagement is a bottleneck | Make self-service onboarding frictionless |
| Regulatory deadlines drive urgency | Build compliance workflows into the product |
| Benchmarking justifies investment | Provide peer comparisons where data exists |
